Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 26, 2026

Potato Eater Productions LLC (“Potato Eater Productions,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit potatoeaterproductions.com (the “Site”) or submit material or correspondence to us. Please read this Policy carefully. By using the Site, you consent to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide voluntarily. When you contact us, submit a script, reel, pitch, or inquiry, or otherwise communicate with us, you may provide personal information including your name, email address, mailing address, phone number, business affiliation, and the contents of your submission and correspondence. You are not required to submit any sensitive information; do not include information you would not want us to receive.

Information collected automatically. Our Site is hosted on GitHub Pages, a service operated by GitHub, Inc. When you visit, GitHub’s infrastructure may automatically log standard technical information including your Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages viewed, dates and times of access, and similar diagnostic data. That information is collected by GitHub under its own privacy practices, available at github.com.

Cookies and similar technologies. We do not set our own cookies on the Site. GitHub Pages may set technical cookies necessary for the secure operation of the hosting platform. As of the Effective Date above, we do not use third-party analytics, advertising trackers, social-media pixels, fingerprinting tools, or behavioral profiling on the Site. If this changes in the future, this Policy will be updated.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to (a) respond to your inquiries and communications; (b) evaluate submissions you have voluntarily sent us; (c) maintain, secure, and improve the Site; (d) administer our business operations; and (e) comply with legal obligations, defend legal claims, and enforce our Terms and Conditions and Submission Release and Agreement.

We do not sell personal information. We do not “share” personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. We do not use your information for targeted advertising or to build advertising profiles.

3. How We Share Information

We may share limited information in the following narrow circumstances:

  • with service providers we engage to operate our business (such as email and file-storage providers), bound by confidentiality and data-protection obligations;
  • with our legal, accounting, financial, or other professional advisors when reasonably necessary;
  • when required by law, subpoena, court order, or other lawful governmental request;
  • to protect the rights, property, or safety of Potato Eater Productions, our personnel, or others, including to investigate or prevent fraud or other unlawful activity;
  • in connection with a corporate transaction such as a merger, acquisition, reorganization, financing, or sale of assets, in which case successor entities will be required to handle your information consistent with this Policy.

4. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, to comply with legal obligations, to resolve disputes, and to enforce our agreements. Where information is no longer needed, we will dispose of it in accordance with our internal retention practices.

5. Data Security

We employ reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. No transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is, however, completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights regarding your personal information, including the rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to our processing of your information; to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent; to receive a portable copy of your data; and to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at contact@potatoeaterproductions.com. We will respond consistent with applicable law.

California residents. California’s Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, grants California residents specific rights regarding their personal information, including the rights to know what we collect, request deletion, request correction, opt out of certain sales or sharing, and not be discriminated against for exercising these rights. As stated above, we do not sell personal information and we do not “share” personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Residents of the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland. If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, the General Data Protection Regulation, the UK GDPR, or comparable laws may apply to our processing. Our legal bases for processing typically include your consent, our legitimate interests in operating and developing our business, the performance of a contract with you, and compliance with legal obligations. You have the rights described above and may contact us to exercise them.

7. Children’s Privacy

The Site and our submission process are not directed to children under the age of thirteen (13), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under thirteen. If you believe a child under thirteen has provided us with personal information, please contact us at contact@potatoeaterproductions.com and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

8. Third-Party Links

The Site may link to third-party websites and services we do not control, including IMDb, project websites such as mickskylark.com, and others. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies. We are not responsible for the content or practices of third-party services. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party site you visit.

9. International Users

The Site is operated from the United States. If you access the Site from outside the United States, you understand and agree that your information may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in the United States, which may have data-protection laws different from those in your jurisdiction.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Effective Date” above and post the updated Policy on the Site. Material changes will be highlighted as reasonably appropriate. Your continued use of the Site after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
